LIS 703: Organization of Knowledge

Final Exam

For the final exam in LIS 703: Organization of Knowledge, I was tasked with demonstrating my knowledge on principles, methods and systems in the organization of all types of library materials and information.This task was accomplished through reflection on course content, along with the creation of MARC records for items provided by the instructor. One of the course objectives for Organization of Knowledge, as per the syllabus, was to “provide an overview of organizational concepts that affect how information can be retrieved/accessed in order to meet user needs in diverse environments (authority work, classification schemes); (Match to SOIS Learning Goals & Outcomes 1b, 2a, 2b, 2c, 3b, 3c)”. Throughout this course, I gained knowledge and skills relating to this outcome, and was able to practice defining, retrieving, and accessing different kinds of information.

Outcome 2b. Explain the impact of information policies on intellectual freedom, access, literacy, information behavior, and other aspects of library and information science.

LIS 701: Core Values, Ethics, and Issues in Library and Information Profession

Discussion Post: On Library Access and Policy

In this discussion post for LIS 701: Core Values, Ethics, and Issues in Library and Information Profession, I compared the policies of two institutions, Bellwood Public Library, and the Chicago Public Library. I chose to compare their policies on unattended children. I found comparing these two policies interesting, as they differed fairly significantly, and that means that the needs in these communities differ. However, it also highlighted the difference between a standalone, smaller library and a larger system of libraries with various branches. This exercise allowed me to consider how smaller library systems may adjust policies based on their population’s needs, and how this impacts access.

Outcome 2c. Develop appropriate responses to assessed information needs within diverse communities/organizations .

LIS748: Collection Management

Core Collection: Juvenile Poetry

In this assignment for LIS 748: Collection Management, I created a juvenile poetry core collection to meet the needs of patrons at Bellwood Public Library. I had to consider the information institution, along with community statistics and information. I also had to consult review sources and selection tools, and explain why I chose each material included in the collection. I considered the demographics of the community and strived to represent these populations, as well as provide a window to authors and stories that may represent a different cultural background. This exercise allowed me to reflect on my own selection biases, and I was able to curate a collection that I could realistically order for this community.
