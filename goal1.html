In this paper, I explored diversity, equity, and inclusion initiatives in the library and information science field. I wrote this paper for the Core Values & Ethics Research Paper assignment in LIS 701: Core Values, Ethics, and Issues in Library and Information Profession. The assignment included a Work in Progress poster, which I included in this portion as well. In this assignment, I researched the issues of diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) in the Library and Information Science field. These issues are reflected in the demographics of information professionals, the demographics of LIS students and educators, library and information science curriculum offerings, community resources, and even cataloging and classification in the field. I examined the state of DEI in the field, successful applications of DEI initiatives, and reflected on ways DEI may be effectively used moving forward. This artifact is a thorough examination of the evolving nature of a core value within diverse information environments, as I explored various information environments and how they are implementing or how they could implement DEI initiatives.

LIS 748: Collection Management

Hot Topic: Climate Change and Library Considerations

This assignment highlights how libraries can implement environmentally conscious practices in the face of climate change while providing current, accurate information on the topic to community members. The bibliography included in this assignment cites various works related to green libraries, climate change literacy, special collections related to climate change, sustainable development practices and challenges, among other considerations. The breadth of resources on climate change in libraries shows how timely this topic is to the profession, while providing information on how it guides practice in various settings.

In April 2024, I attended the annual Public Library Association Conference on behalf of the youth services staff at Bellwood Public Library. I have included the title cards of a few of the educational sessions I attended. I spent three days attending workshops and sessions, and had many conversations with other library professionals about issues and opportunities in the profession. I took materials and ideas from these presentations back to my library and shared them with the rest of my coworkers. We worked to implement reading strategies from what I learned in “The Science of Reading in Public Libraries", and we added some restorative practices in our work with teens that I brought back from “Building Engagement and Changing Futures with Restorative Actions for Teens”.
