Goal 4: Synthesize theory and practice within a dynamic, evolving, and diverse information environment.

Outcome 4a. Apply library and information science theories, principles, and research to professional practice.

LIS 703: Organization of Knowledge

Discussion Post: Ethical Issues in Personal Name Authority Work

In this discussion post for LIS 703: Organization of Knowledge, I explored the issue of personal name authority work, and some of the controversies surrounding the topic. I cited authors who approached the idea of recording gender as a cataloging factor from a queer theory perspective. This is an example of an outside theory applied to professional practice in library and information science, which can complicate cataloging practices. I discussed some issues that may arise when recording gender as a cataloging factor, such as a woman writing under a man’s name, a team writing under one name, or simply someone who does not wish to highlight or list their gender in their work. This discussion emphasizes the importance of considering creators of works, instead of solely focusing on information seekers.

Outcome 4b. Evaluate current and emerging technologies for information capture, organization, dissemination, access, and/or curation to meet the variety of information needs of diverse communities.

LIS 768: Social Media Emerging Technology

Storytelling with Map & Senior Services: Logan Square Throughout The Years

For this outcome, I selected a storytelling project I completed for LIS 768: Social Media Emerging Technology. This course focused on different applications of social media and their impact on the library and information professions and landscape. I decided to use storymap.knightlab.com to create a storymap of my the Logan Square neighborhood in Chicago. This storymap traces different locations in the neighborhood and features notable monuments and locations along with photos and information. Through this assignment, I was able to familiarize myself with the storymap tool, and gain confidence in using this tool in the future with information users.

This website, created as a final project for LIS 753: Internet Fundamentals.

I created this e-portfolio website as my final project for LIS 753: Internet Fundamentals. I decided to showcase the website-building skills I gained through this course in a practical way to create my e-portfolio.
